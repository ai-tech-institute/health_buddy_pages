# Chronic Migraine: When to Seek Expert Care from a Headache Specialist

For those living with chronic migraine, transitioning from general practitioner care to a headache specialist can be a pivotal step toward effective management and improved quality of life. A specialist offers diagnostic precision and access to advanced treatments often unavailable in primary care settings.

**Consider a Headache Specialist if:**

*   **Diagnosis is uncertain or complex:** If your migraines present unusually or with atypical symptoms, a specialist can provide a precise diagnosis.
*   **Current treatments are ineffective:** If several preventive or acute medications haven't significantly reduced your migraine frequency, severity, or duration.
*   **Migraine frequency is high:** You experience 15 or more headache days per month for at least three months, with at least eight being migraine days.
*   **Medication overuse headache (MOH) is suspected:** Overusing acute medications can worsen headaches; a specialist can diagnose MOH and guide detoxification.
*   **You desire advanced treatment options:** Specialists prescribe newer, targeted therapies:
    *   **CGRP Inhibitors** (e.g., Aimovig, Ajovy, Emgality, Vyepti): These FDA-approved preventive medications block pathways involved in migraine, typically considered after other preventives fail. Aimovig, Ajovy, and Emgality are subcutaneous injections (monthly/quarterly), while Vyepti is an intravenous infusion (quarterly). Side effects can include injection site reactions, constipation, muscle cramps, and hypersensitivity.
    *   **Botox Injections:** FDA-approved for chronic migraine prevention, these are for individuals with 15+ headache days/month (8+ migraine days) who haven't responded to other preventives. Administered every 12 weeks via a specific protocol (e.g., PREEMPT) by a trained specialist, potential side effects include neck pain, headache, muscle weakness, dry eyes, or eyelid drooping.
    *   **Neuromodulation Devices:** Non-pharmacological options for acute or preventive relief:
        *   **Cefaly:** Uses external trigeminal nerve stimulation on the forehead (FDA-approved for acute/preventive).
        *   **Nerivio:** Uses remote electrical neuromodulation on the upper arm (FDA-approved for acute/preventive).
        *   **gammaCore:** Uses non-invasive vagus nerve stimulation on the neck (FDA-approved for acute migraine/cluster headache).
        Common side effects include skin irritation, mild dizziness, muscle twitching, or localized discomfort. Usage varies (during an attack or daily for prevention).
*   **Comorbid conditions exist:** Migraine often co-occurs with conditions like depression or anxiety; a specialist can integrate treatment plans.
*   **You are seeking clinical trial participation:** Specialists are often involved in research and can inform you about novel therapies.

**What to Expect from a Headache Specialist:**

A specialist will conduct a thorough neurological exam, delve into your medical history, and develop a personalized treatment plan.

*   **Keep a detailed headache diary:** Before your appointment, track headache frequency, severity, associated symptoms, potential triggers, and the effectiveness/side effects of any medications. This information is invaluable for diagnosis and treatment planning.
*   **Interpreting test results:** Ask specific questions like: "What specific findings from my MRI or blood work are relevant to my migraine diagnosis and treatment?" and "Are there any 'red flags' or concerning findings that need further investigation, or are these results typical for migraine?"

Seeking expert care offers access to advanced tools and interventions, leading to more effective chronic migraine management and a significant improvement in daily life.
