# Chronic Migraine: The Role of Physical Therapy and Exercise

For chronic migraine, physical therapy (PT) and tailored exercise are powerful, often underutilized, tools offering significant benefits for pain reduction, improved function, and enhanced quality of life. This summary provides key insights.

**Understanding the Connection: How Physical Therapy Helps**

Specialized PT addresses cervical spine (neck), temporomandibular joint (TMJ), and postural imbalances linked to migraine. Therapists identify trigger points, muscle tension, and joint restrictions contributing to migraine frequency and intensity.

Key techniques:
*   **Manual Therapy:** Hands-on techniques like massage and mobilization reduce muscle tension and improve joint mobility. Studies suggest cervical dysfunction can decrease migraine days by 2-4 per month and reduce intensity.
*   **Postural Correction:** Therapists guide exercises and ergonomic adjustments to improve posture, reducing migraine triggers.
*   **Craniofacial Therapy:** Specific exercises alleviate jaw tension and associated headaches for TMJ involvement.

**The Power of Prescribed Exercise**

While intense exercise can trigger migraines, a carefully designed, progressive program builds resilience and consistent activity without overstraining.
*   **Aerobic Exercise:** Moderate activities (20-30 minutes, 3-5 times/week) like brisk walking reduce migraine frequency and severity by releasing endorphins and regulating stress.
*   **Strength Training:** Light resistance for neck, shoulder, and core muscles improves stability and reduces imbalances.
*   **Flexibility and Relaxation:** Yoga, Pilates, and stretching reduce stiffness and promote relaxation, vital for migraine management.

**Practical Guidance for Patients**

When considering PT and exercise:
*   **Seek Specialized Care:** Look for PTs specializing in headache/craniofacial pain, ideally with orthopedic manual therapy or neurological PT certifications. Ask about their migraine experience.
*   **Track Progress:** Use a daily migraine diary (frequency, intensity, duration, medication, functional limitations) to share with your PT and doctor.
*   **Manage Expectations:** Benefits accumulate gradually (weeks to months). Communicate openly if progress feels slow or new symptoms arise.
*   **Start Slowly, Progress Gradually:** Avoid sudden intensity increases.
*   **Consistency is Key:** Adherence is crucial for long-term improvement.
*   **Integrate Holistically:** Combine PT/exercise with other migraine strategies.
*   **Insurance/Costs:** Check coverage, co-pays, deductibles, and covered sessions with your insurer.
*   **Cautions:** Discuss any acute neurological symptoms or severe medical conditions with your therapist/doctor, as certain techniques may require caution.

By incorporating PT and tailored exercise, you can actively reduce migraine burden, enhance daily function, and improve overall well-being.
